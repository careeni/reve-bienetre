Natural active ingredients

Argan oil

What is Argan oil ?

Mainly native to Morocco, Argan oil is extracted from the fruit of the Argan tree.

The properties of Argan oil :

Recently introduced for cooking and cosmetic applications, Argan oil is 100% natural. Its biochemical compounds have been recognized as being beneficial to our health.
- Argan oil stimulates the skin. It is therefore excellent for skin elasticity and cell renewal.
- Also beneficial for high blood pressure and the liver, it helps lower bad cholesterol.
- Its anti-aging properties make it ideal for preventing skin aging.
Another advantage : Argan oil helps diminish the appearance of scarring from acne, chickenpox, etc.
- Also useful for relieving joint pain.
Suitable for all skin types, Argan oil is ideal for face and body care treatments, boasting numerous benefits for the hair and nails.

Argan oil in face and body care :

Applied to both face and body, Argan helps restore soft, moisturized and silky smooth skin. In the winter, it calms cutaneous irritation (e.g. eczema) and soothes cracked skin and certain types of burning sensations. Working to prevent skin aging, it is also ideal for warding off wrinkles.

Argan oil in hair care :

Ideal for dry and coarse hair, it helps restore silkiness and shine. How to use : Apply Argan oil along the entire hair length. Wait 30 minutes before applying shampoo and rinse out.

Enjoy this simple recipe for strengthening your nails :

Mix 1 tsp of Argan oil with 1 tsp of lemon juice (or essential oil of lemon). Soak your nails in this solution for 15 minutes. Thoroughly wash hands. Repeat once a week.

Shea butter

What is shea butter ?

A natural treatment for your body, hair and face.

Les propriétés du beurre de karité :

Offering a multitude of reparative properties, it softens and intensely hydrates the skin. It also works as an excellent moisturizer to smooth the cutaneous surface. Rich in vitamins A, D, E and F, as well as in latex and unsaponifiables (preventing transformation into soap), this formula is first and foremost a natural treatment with multiple applications.

Shea butter in face care :

Excellent for face care treatments, shea butter moisturizes and protects the skin, while improving cutaneous elasticity.

Shea butter in body care :

Shea butter is particularly recommended for extremely dry skin types. A few tips for applying shea butter paste to the skin.
- For massage: similarly to moisturizing lotions, shea butter may be massaged into skin to help relax muscles. Use on arms and thighs in case of soreness and stiff joints; the beneficial effect will be immediate.
- Anti-stretchmark action : when pregnant or on a diet, apply shea butter to areas susceptible to weight loss or weight gain (for example thighs) to help prevent the formation of stretchmarks. Please note that this treatment is not intended to eliminate already existing stretchmarks.

Shea butter in hand care :

Many brands use the beneficial properties of shea to formulate their nourishing creams for treating cracked skin and intensely moisturizing hands.

Shea butter in hair care :

This is the ideal revitalizing solution for maintaining resplendently healthy hair, especially when very dry and lackluster. Also perfect for frizzy hair, which is naturally dry.

Barbary fig oil

The properties of Barbary fig oil :

Rich in essential fatty acids and vitamin E, Barbary fig oil ideally performs its anti-aging and anti-wrinkle functions. When used on the skin, it delivers multiple benefits, which include refining and tightening skin texture, diminishing minor spots with a potentially unsightly appearance, regulating sebum and limiting dehydration.

Completely natural, Barbary fig oil may be applied to all parts of the body and is also perfect as massage oil. It contains a high concentration of vitamin E, a well-known antioxidant.

This exquisite oil provides 5 important benefits :

- Antioxidant
- Healing
- Moisturizing
- Nourishing
- Firming
